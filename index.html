Masters by Research

John is a postgraduate research student at University of Leeds, UK, in the Faculty of Environment. His research project is to use AI for landcover classification in dryland environments. He has built a convolutional neural network to classify multispectral satellite imagery from the Sentinel-2 program at 10 m resolution. The neural network is based upon a light convolution network proposed by Song et al. (2019). The neural network was trained on labelled landcover samples (Stanimirova et al. 2023) from global dryland biomes. The accuracy on the test dataset is approximately 85%. The principal software libraries used were Python, Pytorch, Geemap, and Geopandas, in a Jupyter Notebook environment.

The biomes of interest are (from Terrestrial Ecoregions of the World, Olson et al. 2001):

  • Tropical and Subtropical Dry Broadleaf Forests - Biome 2
  • Tropical and Subtropical Grasslands, Savannas, and Shrublands - Biome 7
  • Temparate Grasslands, Savannas, and Shrublands - Biome 8
  • Montane Grasslands and Shrublands - Biome 10
  • Mediterranean Forests, Woodlands, and Scrub - Biome 12
  • Deserts and Xeric Shrublands - Biome 13

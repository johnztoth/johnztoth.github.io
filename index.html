Masters by Research

John is a postgraduate research student at University of Leeds, UK, in the Faculty of Environment. His research project is to use AI for landcover classification in dryland environments. He has built a convolutional neural network to classify multispectral satellite imagery from the Sentinel-2 program at 10 m resolution. The neural network is based upon a light convolution network proposed by Song et al. (2019). The neural network was trained on labelled landcover samples from the GLANCE dataset (Stanimirova et al. 2023) from global dryland biomes (Olson et al. 2001). The accuracy on the test dataset is high, approximately 85%, although the F1-macro score is only 70% due to high class imbalances within the GLANCE dataset. A second neural network was trained (or fine tuned) on the AlphaEarth geospatial foundation model from Google DeepMind (Brown et al. 2025). The accuracy and F1 score of this model are both very high, over 90%, even with the high class imbalances within the dataset. The principal software libraries used were Python, Pytorch, Geemap, and Geopandas, in a Jupyter Notebook environment.

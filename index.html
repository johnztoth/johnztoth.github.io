John Toth

Masters by Research


John is a postgraduate research student at University of Leeds, UK. His research project is to use AI for landcover classification in dryland environments. He is building a convolutional neural network to classify multispectral satellite imagery from the Sentinel-2 program at 10 m resolution. The neural network is trained on labeled landcover samples from global dryland biomes. The principal software libraries used are Pytorch, Geopandas, and Geemap.

The ecosystems that the classification algorithm will be applied to are:

  • Tropical and Subtropical Grasslands, Savannas, and Shrublands
  • Mediterranean Forests, Woodlands, and Scrub
  • Deserts and Xeric Shrublands
  • Montane Grasslands and Shrublands
  • Tropical and Subtropical Dry Broadleaf Forests
